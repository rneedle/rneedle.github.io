Rachel Needle

I am a graphic designer who builds identity and design systems for print and digital platforms.

Currently in the Office of the University Printer (OUP) at Yale University, under the direction of John Gambell. Previously at Potion Design and PERCH Interactive.

Yale University, BA in Art (Graphic Design concentration), Class of 2013.

Yale Bowl Centennial

Yale Athletics asked the University Printer to design a logo to celebrate Yale’s 100th football season in the Yale Bowl stadium. We proposed this logo and an accompanying identity, consisting of nostalgic imagery from Yale’s historical archives, and bold, sporty collegiate type treatments. I have written about the design of this logo here.

Client: Yale Athletics. Art Direction: John Gambell. Design: Rachel Needle.

Human Resources
Engagement Campaign

The University Printer set the tone and designed the communications of a year-long campaign to encourage staff members to be active members of Yale’s campus community and understand the full range of employment benefits. We designed a bright identity and crafted proactive language to be both flexible and consistent across print and digital marketing.

Client: Yale Human Resources. Art Direction: Rachel Needle. Design: Rachel Needle, Emily Monjaraz.

Yale University
Identity Guidelines

In order to better communicate the Yale Identity Guidelines, we established a new website to describe the brand at identity.yale.edu. The office is in the process of rewriting the instructions to make them useful to non-designers and broadening their scope to include basic principles of Yale Design.

Writing: John Gambell, Rachel Needle, Victor Velt. Art Direction: John Gambell, Rachel Needle. Development: Victor Velt, Joanne Piscello.

PERCH Interactive
Retail Displays

PERCH Displays transforms retail tables into interactive shopping experiences. This Display, designed for Kiehl’s Aromatic Blends, uses their advertising campaign and product information to build a narrative that encourages shoppers to learn about and interact with the merchandise. Watch demo here.

Client: Kiehl’s Since 1851. Art Direction: Jared Schiffman. Design and animation: Rachel Needle. Development: Tamar Ziv.

Emergency Management
Guidebook App

To decrease production costs and increase access to the Emergency Management Guidebook, the OUP proposed and designed an iPhone app that translates the current print guidebook to the screen using Adobe Digital Publishing Suite.

Client: Yale Emergency Management. Design: Rachel Needle.

Yale University
Library Newsletters

There are seventeen branches of the Yale University Library, each with its own building, unique collection, and programming. We designed a set of modular newsletters available to the whole system. These newsletters may be branded and customized, from our predetermined ‘kit of parts,’ to suit the needs and character of individual branches. Read more here.

Client: Yale University Library. Art Direction: Rebecca Martz. Design: Rachel Needle. Development: Nick Appleby.

The York Street Muse

The Muse is a serial publication supported by the Sudler Creative and Performing Arts Award. It features the senior theses of the Yale College Creative Writing Concentration. I proposed publishing the work of the Writing Concentrators, designed the seven installments of the 2013 series, and the publication’s logo mark and type.

Design: Rachel Needle. Co-Editors: James Murphy, Rachel Needle.

Yale Posters

The OUP designs posters (32 in x 85 in) to welcome visitors to campus and celebrate University-wide events. The 2013–2014 posters that I designed feature heraldry and patterns in new and modern ways in order to expand upon the visual language of the Yale Identity.

Art Direction: John Gambell. Design: Rachel Needle.

WEEKEND

WEEKEND is the weekly Arts & Leisure magazine published by the Yale Daily News. During my editorship at the YDN, I was responsible for designing the magazine each week and redesigned its website, many elements of which are still in use today.

Design: Rachel Needle. Development: Michael Discala.
